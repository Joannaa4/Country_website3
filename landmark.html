China's Famous Landmarks

The Great Wall of China is a famous landmark. The Great Wall stretches 13,171 miles. Building this Wall consisted of a great army of manpower, composed of soldiers, prisoners, and local people, built the wall.

Tiananmen Square is a large public square in the center of Beijing, China. It has been the site of significant historical events, including the 1989 Tiananmen Square incident, a government crackdown on student demonstrators calling for greater freedom. The square is named after the nearby Tiananmen ("Gate of Heavenly Peace").

Temple of Heaven, large religious complex in the old outer city of Beijing, considered the supreme achievement of traditional Chinese architecture. Its layout symbolizes the belief that heaven is round and earth square. The three buildings are built in a straight line.

The Potala Palace is a dzong fortress in Lhasa, capital of the Tibet Autonomous Region in China. It was the winter palace of the Dalai Lamas from 1649 to 1959, has been a museum since then, and a World Heritage Site since 1994.
